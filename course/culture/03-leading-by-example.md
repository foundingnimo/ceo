People don't listen to your words. They watch:
- What you spend time on
- Who you promote
- What behavior you tolerate

| Work weekends and brag about it | "Work-life balance is lip service" |
| Promote a brilliant jerk | "Results excuse bad behavior" |
| Skip 1:1s with direct reports | "People development isn't a priority" |

## Part 2: The Brilliant Jerk Problem

High performers who violate values present the ultimate culture test.

**The calculation:**
- Keeping them: Short-term output, long-term culture damage
- Firing them: Short-term pain, long-term culture credibility

**The math that matters:** For every brilliant jerk you tolerate, you lose your most values-aligned employees who won't compete on those terms.

**Reed Hastings (Netflix):** "Brilliant jerks are too costly to the effective functioning of a team. Jerky behavior in a colleague is so expensive to deal with that we cut those costs by refusing to have them."

## Part 3: Empowerment vs. Abdication

Empowerment is giving people authority *and* accountability. Abdication is dumping work without support.

| Empowerment | Abdication |
|:------------|:-----------|
| Clear context on goals and constraints | "Figure it out" |
| Access to resources and decisions | No decision rights |
| Defined success metrics | Vague expectations |
| Regular check-ins for support | Disappearing until deadline |
| Celebrating autonomous decisions | Punishing mistakes |

**The empowerment formula:**
1. Share the *why* (strategic context)
2. Define the outcome (not the method)
3. Agree on check-in cadence
4. Get out of the way
5. Give credit; take blame

## Part 4: Managing Your Energy

Your mood is contagious. When you're stressed, anxious, or burned out, the organization feels it.

**Non-negotiables for CEO self-management:**
- Protect thinking time (calendar blocks that aren't negotiable)
- Control your entrance (pause before walking into a room)
- Know your triggers (when do you react badly?)
- Build recovery (not optional; mandatory for sustainable leadership)

## CEO Action Item: The Mirror Exercise

Take 10 minutes:
1. List the top 3 behaviors you *want* to see in your culture.
2. For each: When did you *last* model this behavior publicly?
3. Ask yourself: If everyone behaved exactly like me this week, what culture would we have?

*The honest answer reveals the real culture you're building.*
