## Part 1: Why Diversity = Better Decisions

Research consistently shows:

| Finding | Source |
|:--------|:-------|
| Diverse teams outperform homogeneous ones on complex tasks | Harvard Business Review |
| Companies in top quartile for ethnic diversity are 35% more likely to outperform | McKinsey |
| Diverse groups are better at fact-based decision making | Journal of Personality and Social Psychology |

**Why?** Sameness breeds blind spots. When everyone thinks alike, assumptions go unchallenged.

## Part 2: Diversity vs. Equity vs. Inclusion

These are distinct concepts:

| Term | Definition | Example |
|:-----|:-----------|:--------|
| **Diversity** | Representation of different groups | 40% of engineers are women |
| **Equity** | Fair access to opportunities | Promoting based on merit, not politics |
| **Inclusion** | People feel valued and can contribute | All voices are heard in meetings |

You can have diversity without inclusion (token hires who don't feel welcome). You can have inclusion without diversity (a welcoming monoculture). You need all three.

## Part 3: Common Failure Modes

| Failure | Why It Happens | The Fix |
|:--------|:---------------|:--------|
| **Hire for "culture fit"** | Biases toward sameness | Hire for "culture add"‚Äîwhat's missing? |
| **The "pipeline problem" excuse** | Easy scapegoat | Widen sourcing, partner with diverse networks |
| **DEI as HR's job** | Lets leaders off the hook | CEO owns DEI like any other strategic priority |
| **One-time training** | Checks a box | Embed in hiring, reviews, promotions |
| **No metrics** | Can't improve what you don't measure | Track diversity at every level + inclusion surveys |

## Part 4: Practical DEI Actions

| Area | Action |
|:-----|:-------|
| **Hiring** | Diverse interview panels, blind resume reviews, inclusive job descriptions |
| **Meetings** | Rotate who speaks first, explicitly invite quiet voices, call out interruptions |
| **Advancement** | Audit promotion rates by demographic, sponsor (not just mentor) underrepresented talent |
| **Retention** | Exit interviews to surface patterns, ERGs (Employee Resource Groups) |
| **Accountability** | Include DEI metrics in leadership performance reviews |

## Part 5: What NOT to Do

- **Don't tokenize.** Hiring one person to represent an entire group backfires.
- **Don't expect underrepresented employees to "fix" DEI.** That's leadership's job.
- **Don't prioritize performative gestures.** Social media statements mean nothing without internal change.

## CEO Action Item: The Representation Audit

Take 15 minutes:
1. Write down your leadership team composition (gender, ethnicity, background).
2. Compare to your overall workforce and your customers.
3. Ask: Where are the gaps? Who is *not* in the room when decisions are made?

*If the room looks uniform, you're missing perspectives that matter.*
