# Episode 3: The Job to be Done (Customer Empathy)

**Estimated Time:** 25 Minutes

Why did you buy that specific cup of coffee this morning? Was it because you were a "35-year-old male in a specific zip code"? No. You bought it because you had a **job** that needed doing.

## Part 1: Jobs to be Done (JTBD)
Customers don't buy products; they "hire" them to help them make progress in a specific circumstance.

## Part 2: Getting Out of the Building
You cannot understand the "Job" from your boardroom. You need empathy.
- **Don't ask what they want.** (They'll describe a faster horse).
- **Ask about their struggles.** What were they doing right before they used your product? What was frustrating them?

## CEO Action Item: The Customer "Recall"
Call one of your actual customers this week. Don't pitch them. Ask them:
1. "Try to remember the day you decided to sign up for our product. What was happening in your business that day that made you say 'I need to find a solution for this'?"
2. "What were you using *before* us, and why was it failing you?"

*The answers to these questions are the foundations of your product's true value proposition.*
