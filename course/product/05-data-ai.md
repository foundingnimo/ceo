# Episode 5: Data & AI (The Modern Lever)

**Estimated Time:** 20 Minutes

As CEO, you are probably being told that "AI is the future." It is. But AI for AI's sake is just an expensive distraction. You must use Data and AI to drive **Customer Value.**

## Part 1: Data-Informed vs. Data-Driven
- **Data-Driven:** The numbers tell you what to do. (Dangerous for innovation).
- **Data-Informed:** You use data to identify patterns, but you use human judgment to decide the "Why."

## Top Recommendation: The Root Causes of Product Failure
**Speaker:** Marty Cagan (Silicon Valley Product Group)  
**Event:** Mind the Product San Francisco  
**Link:** Available on Mind the Product's website and Vimeo  
**Duration:** ~50 minutes

**Key Points:**
- Founder of Silicon Valley Product Group, author of "INSPIRED" and "EMPOWERED"
- Former VP of Product at eBay, Netscape
- Discusses 10 root causes of why most products fail
- Emphasizes that half of product ideas don't work and need 3-4 iterations
- Focus on outcomes over outputs, discovery over delivery
- Data is an underutilized source of ideas (often ideas come from executives/customers instead)
- Engineers should be involved early - they're closest to technology and can see what's possible
- Product managers should be experts on users, data, and stakeholders
- Be data-driven/data-informed but balance with intuition and product sense

**Why it's great for a technical CEO:**
- Speaks to the strategic/leadership level rather than tactical execution
- Explains why traditional roadmap-driven approaches fail
- Advocates for empowered product teams using data and experimentation
- Balance of quantitative metrics with qualitative user research

When evaluating a new AI initiative, don't ask "Is it cool?" Ask:
1. **Does this solve a customer problem faster or cheaper?** (Efficiency).
2. **Does this allow us to solve a problem we couldn't solve before?** (New Capability).
3. **Do we have the data to support this, or are we just throwing GPUs at a guess?**

## CEO Action Item: The Metric Audit
Look at the dashboard you review every month.
- Are you looking at **Lagging Indicators** (Revenue, Churn) or **Leading Indicators** (Activation rate of new features, time to first value)?
- Ask your Product team for one "Leading Indicator" that predicts future growth and track that for the next 90 days.
