# Episode 9: Budgeting Fundamentals

**Estimated Time:** 45 Minutes

As your augmentation plan states: "Budgeting and financial forecasting enable strategic planning and resource allocation."

A budget translates your strategy into numbers. It's how you allocate resources to priorities and set expectations for performance.

## Part 1: What Is a Budget?

A budget is a financial plan that:
- **Translates strategy** into numbers
- **Allocates resources** to priorities
- **Sets expectations** for performance
- **Provides benchmarks** for measuring results

## Part 2: Types of Budgets

| Type | Purpose | Timeframe |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **Operating Budget** | Revenue and expense planning | Annual |
| **Capital Budget** | Major investments in assets | Multi-year |
| **Cash Budget** | Cash inflows and outflows | 13-week rolling |
| **Departmental Budgets** | Allocated by team | Annual |

## Part 3: Building an Operating Budget

**Step 1: Revenue Projections**
```
Current ARR: $1,000,000
+ Retained Revenue (90% retention): $900,000
+ New Customers: $400,000
+ Expansion Revenue: $100,000
= Year-End ARR Target: $1,400,000
```

**Step 2: Cost of Goods Sold (Target 15-25% for SaaS)**
- Hosting/Infrastructure: 5-10%
- Customer Support: 5-10%
- Payment Processing: 2-3%

**Step 3: Operating Expenses (Bottoms-Up)**
```

  1 Manager √ó $180K = $180K
  Tools & Software: $50K
  Training: $20K
= Total: $1,000,000
```
