# Episode 8: Cash Flow Management Essentials

**Estimated Time:** 40 Minutes

As your CEO augmentation plan states: "Cash flow management represents perhaps the most critical financial skill for startup CEOs."

A significant percentage of startups fail not because they lack a viable business model, but because they run out of cash before achieving sustainability.

## Part 1: Understanding Your Cash Position

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **Gross Burn** | Total monthly cash outflows | Minimize waste |
| **Net Burn** | Cash outflows - Cash inflows | Trending toward zero |
| **Runway** | Current Cash / Monthly Net Burn | 12-18+ months |

**Example:**
```
Current Cash: $2,000,000
Monthly Revenue: $150,000
Monthly Expenses: $250,000
Net Burn Rate: $100,000/month
Runway: 20 months
```

## Part 2: Accelerating Cash Inflows

| Strategy | How It Works | Impact |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| **Early payment discounts** | 2% discount for payment in 10 days | Faster collection |
| **Invoice immediately** | Don't delay after delivery | Reduces float time |
| **Annual contracts** | Offer discount for annual prepay | Cash upfront |
| **Credit card on file** | Automatic payment collection | Reduces churn |
| **Deposits** | Require deposits for custom work | De-risks projects |

## Part 3: Managing Cash Outflows

| Strategy | How It Works | Consideration |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Extend payables** | Use full payment terms | Don't damage relationships |
| **Scrutinize purchases** | Question every expense | Culture of frugality |
| **Delay non-essentials** | Push out discretionary spend | Don't starve growth |
| **Equity compensation** | Reduce cash needs | Dilution trade-off |
| **Variable costs** | Contractors vs. employees | Flexibility vs. commitment |

## Part 4: 13-Week Rolling Cash Forecast

Essential tool for CEO cash management:

| Week | Starting Cash | Inflows | Outflows | Ending Cash |
|------|---------------|---------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | $500,000 | $80,000 | $120,000 | $460,000 |
| 2 | $460,000 | $75,000 | $115,000 | $420,000 |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| 13 | $xxx | $xxx | $xxx | $xxx |

**Update weekly** based on actuals. Flag weeks where cash gets dangerously low.

## CEO Action Item: Cash Forecast

Create your first 4-week cash forecast:
1. Start with today's cash balance
2. List known inflows (collections, expected payments)
3. List known outflows (payroll, rent, vendors)
4. Identify your lowest cash point
