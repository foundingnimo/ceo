## Part 1: The Financial CEO Mindset

Every decision has financial implications. Always ask:
1. What is the expected return?
2. What is the required investment?
3. What is the risk profile?
4. What are the alternatives?
5. What is the opportunity cost?

## Part 3: Common CEO Financial Decisions

| Decision | Key Questions |
|----------|---------------|
| **Hire vs. Don't Hire** | What revenue/impact will this enable? Fully-loaded cost? Time to productivity? |
| **Build vs. Buy** | Cost to build (time, people)? Cost to buy (price, integration)? Speed to value? |
| **Grow vs. Profit** | ROI on growth investments? Runway needed? Can we grow efficiently? |
| **Raise vs. Bootstrap** | Capital needed? Dilution acceptable? Growth upside worth it? |

## Part 4: The Trade-off Triangle

```
        Growth
         /\
        /  \
       /    \
      /      \
Profitability --- Cash
```

**You can optimize for two, but usually sacrifice the third.**

## Part 5: Financial Red Flags

| Red Flag | Meaning | Action |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Declining gross margin | Pricing pressure | Review pricing |
| Increasing CAC | Harder to acquire | Audit channels |
| Decreasing NRR | Customers leaving | Improve product |
| Rising burn without growth | Inefficient spending | Cut costs |
| Runway < 6 months | Survival risk | Fundraise NOW |

## Part 6: Building Financial Intuition

**Practice these habits:**
1. Review financials weekly
2. Ask "what's the ROI?" for every investment
3. Model scenarios before big decisions
4. Compare your metrics to benchmarks
5. Talk regularly with your CFO/finance team
