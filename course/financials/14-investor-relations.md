# Episode 14: Investor Communications & Fundraising

**Estimated Time:** 40 Minutes

For funded or fundraising CEOs, translating financial performance into compelling investor communications is essential.

## Part 1: What Investors Evaluate

| Category | Key Questions |
|----------|---------------|
| **Market** | TAM, growth, timing |
| **Business Model** | Unit economics, margins, path to profit |
| **Growth** | Revenue trajectory, efficiency |
| **Team** | Experience, financial acumen |

## Part 2: Metrics Investors Want by Stage

**Seed / Series A:**
- MRR/ARR and growth rate
- Customer acquisition and retention
- Burn rate and runway
- Team and hiring plans

**Series B+:**
- Net revenue retention
- LTV:CAC and payback
- Gross and operating margins
- Cash efficiency
- Path to profitability

## Part 4: Term Sheet Basics

**Key Terms:**
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Pre-money** | Value before investment |
| **Post-money** | Pre-money + investment |
| **Dilution** | Reduction in ownership % |
| **Liquidation Preference** | Payout order in exit |

**Example:**
```
Pre-money: $10M
Investment: $5M
Post-money: $15M
Investor ownership: $5M / $15M = 33.3%
```

## Part 5: The Board Financial Package

Your monthly/quarterly package should include:
1. Financial statements (P&L, BS, CF)
2. Budget vs. actual comparison
3. Key metrics dashboard
4. Cash position and runway
5. Customer metrics (MRR, churn, NRR)
6. Operational highlights
7. Updated forecast (if changed)

**Send materials 3-5 days before board meetings.**

## CEO Action Item: Investor Pitch

Prepare a 2-minute financial summary covering:
1. Current ARR and growth rate
2. Unit economics (LTV:CAC)
3. Runway and fundraising timeline
4. Path to profitability
