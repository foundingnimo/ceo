## Part 1: Know Your Investors

### Key Insight: Fund Dynamics Matter

Your VC investor isn't just an individualâ€”they're part of a fund with its own pressures:

Ask your investors: "How does our company fit into your fund's portfolio? What does success look like for you?"

## Part 4: Handling Difficult Conversations

Sometimes you need to deliver bad news. Here's how:

| Situation | How to Handle |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Missed targets** | Own it immediately. Explain why, what you learned, what you're changing. |
| **Key person leaving** | Proactive call > email. Share your succession plan. |
| **Major pivot** | Frame as learning, not failure. Show the new opportunity clearly. |
| **Runway concerns** | Give maximum lead time. Share options and your recommendation. |
| **Unexpected competition** | Acknowledge it. Show why you're still positioned to win. |

**The Script for Bad News:**
1. State the issue directly (no burying)
2. Explain the context (what happened, why)
3. Take ownership (even if external factors played a role)
4. Share the plan (what you're doing about it)
5. Ask for support (specific help you need)
