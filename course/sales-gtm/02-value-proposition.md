## Part 2: The Buyer Committee

In B2B, you're not selling to one person. Each stakeholder cares about different things:

| Buyer Type | Their Question | What They Care About |
|:-----------|:---------------|:---------------------|
| **Economic Buyer** (CFO, CEO) | "What's the ROI?" | Cost savings, revenue impact, payback period |
| **Technical Buyer** (CTO, IT) | "Will it work?" | Integration, security, maintenance burden |
| **Functional Buyer** (VP Sales, Marketing) | "Will it solve my problem?" | Workflow fit, team adoption, specific pain relief |
| **End User** | "Is it easy to use?" | UX, learning curve, daily friction |

You need messaging for *each* of these personas.

## Part 3: The Positioning Statement

Use this template to create crisp positioning:

> **For** [target customer] **who** [has this problem], **our product is a** [category] **that** [key benefit]. **Unlike** [competitor/alternative], **we** [key differentiator].

**Example (Slack):**
> For teams who struggle with email overload, Slack is a messaging platform that makes work communication instant and searchable. Unlike email, we organize conversations by topic and integrate with 2,000+ tools.
