## Part 2: The Decision Matrix

Use this to evaluate your fit:

| Factor | Favors PLG | Favors SLG |
|:-------|:-----------|:-----------|
| **ACV** | <$10K/year | >$25K/year |
| **Buyer** | End user can buy | Committee decision |
| **Complexity** | Self-serve onboarding | Needs implementation |
| **Sales Cycle** | Days to weeks | Months |
| **Competition** | Trying before competitor | Relationship-driven |

Most B2B SaaS companies end up **Hybrid**: PLG for SMB acquisition + Sales for enterprise conversion.

## Part 4: The Founder-Led Sales Trap

Every company starts with founder-led sales. The trap is staying there too long.

**Signs you're stuck:**
- CEO/founder is still closing majority of deals
- No documented sales process exists
- New sales hires can't replicate founder success
- Pipeline depends on founder's network

**The transition path:**
1. Document every step of how *you* sell
2. Hire a senior AE who can operate the playbook
3. Measure and iterate until they can close without you
4. Only then, scale the team

## CEO Action Item: The GTM Audit

Answer these questions honestly:
1. What is your current primary motion? (PLG / SLG / Founder-led chaos)
2. Based on your ACV and buyer, is this the *right* motion?
3. If you're still founder-led: What's the one thing blocking you from hiring your first dedicated seller?

*Write down your answers. This clarity determines your next 6 months of GTM investment.*
