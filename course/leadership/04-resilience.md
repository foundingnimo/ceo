## Part 2: Physical Foundations

Resilience starts with the body. These aren't luxuriesâ€”they're performance essentials.

### Sleep

| Sleep Deprivation Effect | CEO Impact |
| :--- | :--- |
| Impaired judgment | Poor decisions on high-stakes matters |
| Reduced emotional regulation | Reactive, short-tempered |
| Decreased creativity | Can't see solutions |
| Lower immune function | Getting sick at the worst times |

**Minimum viable sleep:** 7 hours, consistently. Not 5 hours with "catch-up" on weekends.

### Exercise

| Benefit | Mechanism |
| :--- | :--- |
| Stress reduction | Burns cortisol |
| Mental clarity | Increases blood flow to brain |
| Energy | Counterintuitive but true |
| Sleep improvement | Deeper, more restorative sleep |

**Minimum viable exercise:** 30 minutes, 4x per week. Schedule it like your most important meeting.

### Nutrition

The CEO lifestyle makes healthy eating hard. Focus on damage control:
- Reduce alcohol (disrupts sleep, adds to stress)
- Limit caffeine after noon
- Don't skip meals (blood sugar crashes = bad decisions)
- Stay hydrated

## Part 3: Mental Practices

Beyond physical foundations, mental practices build psychological resilience.

### Meditation/Mindfulness

| Practice | Time | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Morning meditation | 10-20 min | Sets calm tone for day |
| Breathing exercises | 2 min as needed | Immediate stress relief |
| Walking without phone | 15-30 min | Mental reset |

You don't need to become a monk. Even 10 minutes of daily meditation builds noticeable resilience over weeks.

### Perspective-Taking

When stressed, ask:
- "Will this matter in 5 years?"
- "What would I advise a friend in this situation?"
- "What's the worst that actually happens if this goes wrong?"

Most crises are survivable. Perspective reduces their emotional weight.

### Gratitude Practice

Sounds soft, but research confirms: gratitude rewires the brain toward resilience.
- End each day with 3 things that went well
- Periodically thank team members specifically

## Part 4: Recognizing Burnout Warning Signs

Burnout doesn't announce itself. It creeps up. Know the warning signs:

| Early Signs | Later Signs |
| :--- | :--- |
| Dreading Monday | Constant exhaustion, even after rest |
| Increasing cynicism | Detachment from work you used to love |
| Difficulty focusing | Physical symptoms (headaches, illness) |
| Shorter temper | Wanting to quit or escape |
| Skipping recovery time | Depression, hopelessness |

### The Burnout Trap

Many CEOs respond to burnout signs by working harder:
- "I'll push through this quarter, then rest"
- "If I just close this deal/round/hire..."
- "I can't afford to slow down now"

This accelerates the crash. Recognize the trap.

### When to Get Help

| Situation | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| Occasional stress | Normalâ€”use practices from this episode |
| Persistent symptoms for weeks | Talk to someone (coach, therapist, trusted peer) |
| Physical symptoms | See a doctor |
| Feeling hopeless or trapped | Professional support immediately |

There's no weakness in seeking help. It's a strategic investment in your most critical asset: you.

## Part 5: Building a Support System

CEOs often feel they can't be vulnerable with anyone. This isolation is dangerous.

### Your Support Network

| Role | Purpose | Who |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Executive coach** | Professional development, accountability | Paid relationship |
| **Therapist** | Mental health, processing | Paid relationship |
| **Peer CEO group** | Shared experiences, advice | YPO, EO, informal groups |
| **Personal board** | Life advice, perspective | 3-5 trusted people |
| **Spouse/partner** | Emotional support | (Boundary: don't make them your only outlet) |

### Finding Your People

- CEO peer groups (YPO, Vistage, EO)
- Industry-specific CEO dinners
- Former CEOs who've "been there"
- Executive coaches who specialize in founders

**The investment:** A coach or therapist typically costs $500-1500/month. This is a rounding error in your compensation and can be career-saving.
